Brescia

The Lioness of Italy

Introduction

Brescia is a city in eastern Lombardy, located between Lake Garda and the Po Valley. It is known as the Lioness of Italy for its role during the Ten Days of 1849. Today, it blends history and modern life.

Location

Brescia lies in a strategic area between Milan and Verona, a position that has supported its cultural and economic development over time.

History

Founded by the Romans as Brixia, Brescia still preserves important traces of its ancient past. Over the centuries, it was shaped by Lombard and Venetian rule. During the Italian Risorgimento, the city became a symbol of resistance.

Past and present share the same space

Brescia Today

Brescia is a modern and dynamic city, with a strong industrial tradition and an active cultural scene.

Facts

  • Known as the Lioness of Italy
  • Roman monuments in the historic center
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Strong industrial identity

Conclusion

Brescia is a city where history remains part of everyday life.
